High-Temperature Hydrolysis & Impact Resistant Casting Alloys

Flexilon RS-HT Series

Advanced polymer alloys designed to bridge the gap between high-performance elastomers and engineering thermoplastics. Originally engineered for the extreme demands of the offshore energy sector, the RS-HT Series addresses the two critical failure modes in heavy industrial casting: hydrolytic degradation in hot/wet environments and core burning in massive sections.

The Technology

"Self-Stabilising" Hydrophobic Matrix with dual-protection mechanism

Standard polyurethane systems are typically hygroscopic—they absorb water which attacks the polymer backbone at temperatures above 80°C, leading to catastrophic reversion. The RS-HT Series utilises a unique dual-protection mechanism that fundamentally changes how the material interacts with moisture.

1Proprietary Hydrophobic Matrix

The alloy is engineered with a specialised backbone architecture that naturally repels water absorption at the molecular level. This "water-shielding" effect prevents the saturation that typically triggers degradation.

2Epoxide Scavenging Phase

A reactive epoxide component is incorporated into the network. As urethane bonds undergo initial hydrolysis, the epoxide phase reacts with the degradation byproducts to re-crosslink the network in real-time.

Key Advantages

  • Molecular-level water resistance
  • Active re-crosslinking mechanism
  • Extended service life in wet conditions
  • Maintains mechanical properties over time

Massive Section Casting

Manufacturing advantage for large industrial parts

A major limitation of casting large industrial parts (Paper Rolls, Impellers) is the "Mass Effect," where chemical exotherm burns the core of the product. The RS-HT Series phase-separated cure profile overcomes this fundamental limitation.

Processing Compatibility

Drop-in compatibility with standard low-pressure dispensing machinery including Baulé, Cannon, and Sulpol equipment. No specialised processing equipment required.

Mechanical & Impact Stability

High-modulus structural alloy with long-term dimensional stability

Unlike standard rubber which relies on elasticity to absorb impact, the RS-HT Series (specifically RS-3604 and RS-3264) functions as a High-Modulus Structural Alloy.

Dimensional Stability: The material resists deformation under heavy nip loads or centrifugal force, maintaining strict component geometry throughout service life.

Zero Embrittlement: Critical for impact applications, the material retains its elongation and toughness properties long-term. Stress-strain analysis confirms no loss of ductility after 12 months of thermal exposure.
